Mahjong physical AI

2018(Ongoing)

The current project I'm working on consists on an AI that enables a laptop to play mahjong from the video feedback of its webcam. Right now, the only part which has been tackled is the computer vision part. More specifically, detecting the position and orientation of tiles on an image, as well as detecting different zones of the screen such as the hand of the player or their respective discards. For this part, an unsupervised approach was taken (to avoid having to manually label a dataset) and a clustering of embeddings is able to obtain good results on tile detection and zone detection phases. Exact tile classification is still a problem which may be solved with a supervised CNN. All the project is done with python, tensorflow and jupyter.

March Wars

2015

A 12 developer 12 artist project done in the ES course of Universitat de Barcelona. The end result is a traditional RTS with some warhammer mechanics added. In this project we used agile methodologies (particularly scrum) and it was a trial by fire for our git knowledge. I mostly worked on the AI and the FOW (Fog of War). The game was done with C# and Unity. The code is open source and can be found at https://github.com/eloipuertas/ES2015A. Chess+: The revenge of minimax

2014

A game of chess where you are able to customize your starting pieces, either by changing the position or replacing them for pieces of other board games. You can play against a friend online or with an AI. I started this project when I learnt about algorithms such as minimax, and naively thought that only by implementing those I would have a decent chess engine. After running into problems such as Horizon Effect, board representation limitation and limited computing resources, I had to delve a lot deeper into traditional chess engines to obtain a decent AI. This game was entirely done in C# and XNA, the online matches were P2P.

ArenaSmash

2012

The most copyright infringing of games. Free for all battles for up to 8 players online. You could choose from up to 16 characters with unique abilities. Most of the characters were from famous games or shows at the time. The sprites I used were usually taken from the originals, which makes releasing this absolutely ilegal. The game was entirely done in C# and XNA, the online matches used a master server to relay the packets and do some basic cheat protection.
